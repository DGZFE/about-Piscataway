Places to Visit

  • East Jersey Old Town Village

    Description: Step back in time at East Jersey Old Town Village, a living history museum that recreates life in the 18th century. Explore historic buildings, interact with costumed interpreters, and experience the daily life of early European settlers in New Jersey.

  • Rutgers Gardens

    Description: Explore the beauty of Rutgers Gardens, a 180-acre botanical garden with diverse plant collections, scenic trails, and educational programs. Enjoy the tranquility of the bamboo forest, admire the colorful flower displays, and learn about sustainable gardening practices.

  • Johnson Park

    Description: Enjoy a day outdoors at Johnson Park, a sprawling park with picnic areas, playgrounds, sports fields, and walking trails. Take a leisurely stroll along the Raritan River, have a family picnic, or enjoy a game of soccer or baseball.

  • Piscatawaytown Burial Ground

    Description: Visit the historic Piscatawaytown Burial Ground, the final resting place of many of Piscataway's early settlers. The burial ground dates back to the 17th century and provides a glimpse into the lives of the town's founders.

  • The Cornelius Low House

    Description: Explore the elegant Cornelius Low House, a historic mansion built in 1741. The house is now a museum showcasing 18th-century architecture and decorative arts. Take a guided tour and learn about the Low family and their role in Piscataway's history.

  • Metlar-Bodine House Museum

    Description: Discover the history of the Metlar-Bodine House Museum, a historic house museum built in the 18th century. The museum features exhibits on local history, decorative arts, and the lives of the families who lived in the house.

  • Raritan Center Business Park

    Description: Explore the bustling Raritan Center Business Park, one of the largest industrial parks in the United States. The park is home to a variety of businesses, including warehouses, distribution centers, and manufacturing facilities.
